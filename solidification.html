No Breaks
After the 2016 Finals win, the Cavaliers were looking to repeat. However, on July 7, 2016, all-star and debatably the second best player in the world right between LeBron James and Steph Curry, Kevin Durant joined the Golden State Warriors. This meant that Durant joined fellow stars Curry, Thompson, Green, and Iguodala. Surrounded by a good supporting cast, it was looking quite unwinnable for Cleveland.
Inevitable Loss
And unwinnable it was, the Warriors beat the Cavaliers 4-1. Despite commendable efforts by LeBron and Kyrie, both averaging 30 points per game. The best team ever assembled was understandably too much for them.
LeBronto the One Man Team
The Cavaliers had just lost its second best player, and arguably the second best point guard in the world. To fill a hole this big was impossible.. Despite LeBron's efforts, averaging almost a 34-point triple double, he just couldn't carry this horrible supporting cast to the championship as they fell in the NBA Finals in 4-0 sweep fashion to the Warriors. However, this could be considered as the greatest playoff performance anyone has ever had, and his insane dominance, specifically over the Toronto Raptors, hitting fadeaways left and right and that impossible floater earned him the nickname "LeBronto"
Shift in Power
In the 2018 offseason, the biggest bombshell in free agency was LeBron James' decision to join the Los Angeles Lakers. This caused a huge shift in power with the best player in the world moving to the opposite conference, the eastern conference was open and ready for the taking of those who dare. Meanwhile, LeBron continued to grow his brand beyond basketball in Los Angeles.
Anthony Davis & Bubble Chip
As if the Cavaliers couldn't get any unluckier, in Game 1 of the 2015 NBA Finals, Kyrie Irving fell down to the floor with 2 minutes left in overtime and injured his knee. First, the big man and now the floor general.. It seems as though everything that could go wrong went wrong. This will forever be seen as a day Cavalier fans would never want to remember, especially knowing that the Cavaliers had a legitimate chance of winning the championship that season.
Timeless
Today, the lakers sit at 0-5 at the bottom of the NBA standings. However, this is something you can't blame LeBron James for, especially because he is averaging 26-7-9 but as usual, LeBron is alone having a walking brick in the washed Russell Westbrook and the always injured Anthony Day-to-Dayvis. It's his 20th season and he is still a top player in the world. LeBron continues to show true greatness and never seen before longetivity, as he further solidifies his legacy.
